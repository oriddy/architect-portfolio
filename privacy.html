Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 January 2024

Webb Studio Architecture LLC ("Webb Studio", "we", "us" or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and safeguard your personal information when you visit webbstudio.com (the "Site") or engage our professional services.

Please read this Policy carefully. By using the Site or engaging our services, you consent to the practices described herein.

1. Overview

We collect only the information necessary to provide you with an excellent experience and to deliver our professional services. We do not sell personal data to third parties. We take your privacy seriously and apply appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Contact information: name, email address, phone number, postal address.
  • Project information: details about your project, budget, timeline and preferences that you share with us.
  • Communications: records of correspondence, including emails, form submissions and meeting notes.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, time spent on pages and referring URLs — collected automatically via cookies and analytics tools.
  • Financial information: billing details necessary to process payments (handled securely by our payment processors; we do not store card details).

We collect information directly from you (through forms and correspondence), automatically (through cookies and analytics), and occasionally from third parties (such as referral partners or public records).

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide professional services.
  • To manage our business relationship with you as a client.
  • To process payments and maintain financial records.
  • To communicate project updates, invoices and relevant information.
  • To improve the Site's content, performance and user experience.
  • To comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations.
  • To send service-related communications (not marketing, unless you opt in).

Our legal bases for processing under GDPR (where applicable) include: performance of a contract, legitimate interests, compliance with legal obligations, and consent.

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal information. We may share it with:

  • Service providers: third-party companies that assist us with IT systems, accounting, legal services and project management tools, bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Professional collaborators: engineers, contractors or consultants involved in your project, only to the extent necessary.
  • Legal requirements: when required by law, court order or regulatory authority.
  • Business transfers: in the event of a merger, acquisition or sale of assets, with notice provided to affected users.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience on the Site. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential cookies: required for the Site to function (session management, security).
  • Analytics cookies: help us understand how visitors use the Site (e.g., Google Analytics). Data is anonymised where possible.
  • Preference cookies: remember your settings and choices.

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect certain Site functionality. For more information, please visit allaboutcookies.org.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. Specifically:

  • Client project records: retained for 7 years after project completion (for legal and warranty purposes).
  • Enquiry data for non-clients: retained for up to 2 years.
  • Analytics data: retained in accordance with the provider's policy (typically 26 months).

When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration or destruction. These include:

  • Encrypted transmission via HTTPS/TLS.
  • Access controls limiting who can access personal data.
  • Regular security assessments of our systems and processes.

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Restriction: request that we limit processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Objection: object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@webbstudio.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Children's Privacy

Our Site and services are not directed to children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

10. International Data Transfers

Webb Studio operates from offices in the United States, the United Kingdom and Japan. If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries that may not offer the same level of data protection as your home country.

Where such transfers occur, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission or adequacy decisions, as applicable.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Where changes are material, we will provide more prominent notice (e.g., by email to registered clients). Your continued use of the Site after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.

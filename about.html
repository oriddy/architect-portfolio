About the studio

We design
meaning.

Architecture is the art of creating spaces that shape how we live, work and feel. At Webb Studio, we have spent 24 years pursuing that art with rigour, curiosity and care.

Marcus Webb

Every project begins with a single question:
what does this place want to become?

Marcus Webb studied architecture at Yale before completing his postgraduate studies at the Architectural Association in London. He spent the early years of his career at Renzo Piano Building Workshop in Paris, where he developed a deep reverence for materiality and the poetics of structure.

In 2000, he returned to New York and founded Webb Studio with a belief that architecture should be both intellectually rigorous and profoundly human. The practice has since grown to a team of 38 architects, designers and specialists working across the United States, Europe and Asia.

Marcus is a Fellow of the American Institute of Architects, a visiting professor at Columbia GSAPP, and the author of The Weight of Light (Phaidon, 2019), a meditation on materiality in contemporary architecture.

Career milestones

A Studio
History

2000

Webb Studio founded

Established in a single-room office in SoHo, New York, with a team of three architects and a vision to change how space is experienced.

2005

First major public commission

The Riverside Community Library, Brooklyn — a 12,000 sq ft public building that established the studio's civic reputation.

2010

London studio opens

Expansion into Europe with a new studio in Clerkenwell, London, enabling work on major international projects.

2014

AIA Fellow designation

Marcus Webb elected Fellow of the American Institute of Architects in recognition of his contributions to the profession.

2018

Tokyo office established

A third studio opened in Tokyo, completing a global network and enabling deep engagement with Japanese architectural culture.

2023

Cascade Residence — AIA Award

The Malibu residence wins the AIA National Excellence Award, confirming the studio's position at the forefront of contemporary residential design.
